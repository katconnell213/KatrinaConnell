My Teaching Philosophy

While I don't currently teach in a classroom, I find many ways to share the knowledge and skills I have acquired with others, and it is one of this things about my work that brings me the greatest joy. Nothing makes me smile harder than showing someone the one trick they needed to make something work or the time saving method to complete a large task in record time. I love sharing information with people and seeing them take it and run with it. Below you will find a teaching philosophy statement for when I teach in a classroom setting, but my general teaching philosophy can be broken down in three simple components.

  1. Provide background information and materials in multiple formats:
    I prefer a combination of video and written media which allows everyone a chance to slow down material or re-review it, which helps everyone, not only second language speakers.
  2. Hands on is best
    Whether I'm in a classroom or the lab, I believe everyone understand concepts and procedures better when they do it themselves, so I always emphasis hands on activities.
  3. Scaffold learning and high stakes environments
    Learning procedures with expensive equipment can put people on edge and make them nervous. Though a combination of hands on practice and scaffolding independence with "safety nets" such as calibrating an eye-tracker yourself (but with the lab manager in the room ready to help if needed), can allow students to prove to themselves that they know what they are doing.
